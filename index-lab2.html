Almaty — beautiful, hospitable city with an amazing landscape.

Almaty has long ceased to be the capital of Kazakhstan, but remains an important financial and cultural center of the republic. This is one of the most beautiful cities in Central Asia. It is located at the foot of the Trans-Ili Alatau - the northern ridge of the Tien Shan.

Almaty attracts tourists primarily with its high mountains and rich cultural and historical heritage. There are many attractions in and around the city. There is a place for everyone to like.

Recreation

The Medeu is an outdoor speed skating and bandy rink. It is located in a mountain valley (Medeu Valley, or the valley of Malaya Alma-Atinka River) on the south-eastern outskirts of Almaty, Kazakhstan.


Şymbūlaq is a ski resort near Almaty, located in the upper part of the Medeu Valley in the Zailiisky Alatau mountain range, at the elevation of 2,200 metres (7,200 ft) above sea level.


Big Almaty Lake is a natural lake located in Trans-Ili Alatau mountains on 2511 above the sea level near Almaty (15 km South from Almaty). Like a majority of lakes in Trans-Ili Alatau, this lake formed as the result of an earthquake. The lake is a major source of drinking water for the region. People can access the lake by car (approximately 1 hour drive from the city center), bike, or hiking (approximately a half-day trip).
